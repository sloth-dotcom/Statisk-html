Flow 2 · Trial & Subscription Conversion

Four sub-flows turn intent into payment: trials into subscriptions, frequent credit buyers into subscribers, low balances into top-ups, and renewals into retained revenue — including a dunning sequence.

Primary KPI
Trial→paid %, dunning recovery %
Benchmark
15–25% / 40–60%
Emails
18
Entry trigger
Multiple — trial start, credit usage, renewal nearing, payment failed.
Audience
Trials, credit buyers, subscribers.
Cadence
18 emails across 4 sub-flows.
Exit criteria
Per sub-flow — see below.
Delivery
Mix of Marketing Automation and event-triggered API sends.
Suppression group
Billing & account, Offers & promotions.

Sub-flows

2A · Trial-to-paid
6 emails

Assumes a 14-day trial. A usage-aware countdown: show people what they’ve already done, then what they’d lose. 3 emails during the trial, 3 after expiry.

2B · Credit → subscription upsell
3 emails

Frequent credit buyers are leaving money on the table — and exposed to friction every time they hit zero. This sub-flow makes the maths obvious.

2C · Credit replenishment
2 emails

Real-time and short. Remove friction before it interrupts a project — and quietly surface the subscription where it would be cheaper.

2D · Renewal protection & failed-payment recovery
7 emails

Two tracks: the renewal track reframes the bill as a value recap; the dunning track recovers failed payments over 10 days before an account pauses. Dunning emails are account-critical and bypass marketing opt-out.
